## 3. Decision Logic

The final decision is based on **aggregate IA and GA scores**:

### Formula:
```
Total_IA = Sum of scores for criteria categorized as "IA" (max 6)
Total_GA = Sum of scores for criteria categorized as "GA" (max 4)

IF Total_IA >= 5 THEN
    Decision = "Accepted"
ELSE IF (Total_GA > 2.5 AND Total_GA < 5) THEN
    Decision = "Accepted with Revision"
ELSE
    Decision = "Rejected"
END IF
```

### Decision Outcomes:
1. **Accepted**  
   - Requires: **Total IA ≥ 5** (out of 6)  
   - Interpretation: Strong problem identification, literature survey, and presentation quality  
   - Next Steps: Proceed to Phase 1 implementation

2. **Accepted with Revision**  
   - Requires: **Total IA < 5 AND (2.5 < Total GA < 5)**  
   - Interpretation: Weak IA components but moderate GA components; objectives and SDG alignment need refinement  
   - Next Steps: Address IA feedback; resubmit revised proposal within 7 days

3. **Rejected**  
   - Requires: **Total IA < 5 AND (Total GA ≤ 2.5 OR Total GA ≥ 5)**  
   - Interpretation: Major deficiencies in problem formulation, literature review, or objectives  
   - Next Steps: Rework proposal from scratch; resubmit in next review cycle

## 4. Evaluation Criteria Details

### Criterion 1: Problem Identification & Formulation (IA)
**Maximum Score:** 2 points  
**Weight:** 20% of total score

**What is Evaluated:**
- Clarity and specificity of the problem statement
- Relevance to the domain (customer support knowledge management)
- Feasibility of addressing the problem within project scope
- Alignment with organizational needs (internal knowledge management vs. external support)

**Scoring Breakdown:**
- **2.0 (Excellent):** Problem statement is crystal clear, highly relevant, technically feasible, and well-aligned with industry needs. Includes quantified pain points (e.g., "Support agents spend 40% of time searching for answers").
- **1.5 (Good):** Problem statement is clear and relevant; minor gaps in quantification or domain alignment.
- **1.0 (Satisfactory):** Problem statement is understandable but lacks specificity or quantified impact.
- **0.5 (Needs Improvement):** Problem statement is vague; relevance or feasibility is questionable.
- **0.0 (Unsatisfactory):** No coherent problem statement provided.

---

### Criterion 2: Literature Survey / Market and Patent Analysis (IA)
**Maximum Score:** 2 points  
**Weight:** 20% of total score

**What is Evaluated:**
- Breadth and depth of reviewed literature (academic papers, industry reports, existing tools)
- Ability to connect existing work to the problem domain
- Identification of **technical gaps** (e.g., "No existing RAG system supports multi-tenant vector isolation")
- Quality of gap analysis (specificity, relevance, actionability)

**Scoring Breakdown:**
- **2.0 (Excellent):** Comprehensive survey of 5+ relevant sources; clear identification of 3+ technical gaps with specific examples; demonstrates deep understanding of state-of-the-art.
- **1.5 (Good):** Good survey of 4-5 sources; identifies 2-3 gaps; minor weaknesses in depth or gap specificity.
- **1.0 (Satisfactory):** Adequate survey of 3-4 sources; identifies 1-2 gaps; lacks depth or specific examples.
- **0.5 (Needs Improvement):** Shallow survey (<3 sources); vague or irrelevant gap identification.
- **0.0 (Unsatisfactory):** No literature survey or no gap analysis provided.

---

### Criterion 3: Presentation & Documentation Quality (IA)
**Maximum Score:** 2 points  
**Weight:** 20% of total score

**What is Evaluated:**
- Clarity and organization of presentation slides (logical flow, visual hierarchy)
- Professionalism of documentation (grammar, formatting, consistency)
- Effectiveness of visual aids (diagrams, charts, tables)
- Adherence to submission guidelines (file formats, naming conventions, completeness)

**Scoring Breakdown:**
- **2.0 (Excellent):** Flawless presentation with professional visuals; documentation is publication-ready; all guidelines followed.
- **1.5 (Good):** Well-organized presentation; minor issues with formatting or visuals; documentation is clear.
- **1.0 (Satisfactory):** Acceptable presentation; some organizational issues; documentation meets minimum standards.
- **0.5 (Needs Improvement):** Disorganized presentation; poor visual quality; documentation has multiple errors.
- **0.0 (Unsatisfactory):** Unreadable or incomplete presentation/documentation.

---

### Criterion 4: Research Objectives / Product Goals Formulation (GA)
**Maximum Score:** 2 points  
**Weight:** 20% of total score

**What is Evaluated:**
- Clarity of objectives (specific, unambiguous language)
- Measurability (quantified success criteria, e.g., "Reduce search time by 70%")
- Relevance to problem statement and gap analysis
- Achievability within project timeline and resources

**Scoring Breakdown:**
- **2.0 (Excellent):** Objectives are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound); directly address identified gaps; include clear success metrics.
- **1.5 (Good):** Objectives are clear and measurable; minor gaps in specificity or alignment with gaps.
- **1.0 (Satisfactory):** Objectives are understandable but lack measurability or clear alignment.
- **0.5 (Needs Improvement):** Objectives are vague; measurability or relevance is weak.
- **0.0 (Unsatisfactory):** No clear objectives provided.

---

### Criterion 5: Alignment with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) (GA)
**Maximum Score:** 2 points  
**Weight:** 20% of total score

**What is Evaluated:**
- Identification of relevant UN SDGs (primary and secondary)
- Depth of alignment analysis (how the project contributes to SDG targets)
- Quantified societal impact (e.g., "Improve organizational productivity by 70%")
- Credibility of impact claims (realistic, evidence-based)

**Scoring Breakdown:**
- **2.0 (Excellent):** Strong alignment with 2+ primary SDGs; quantified impact with credible metrics; clear contribution to SDG targets.
- **1.5 (Good):** Good alignment with 1-2 SDGs; some quantified impact; minor gaps in depth or credibility.
- **1.0 (Satisfactory):** Acceptable alignment with 1 SDG; limited quantification or shallow analysis.
- **0.5 (Needs Improvement):** Weak alignment; no quantified impact; SDG selection seems forced.
- **0.0 (Unsatisfactory):** No SDG alignment discussed.
