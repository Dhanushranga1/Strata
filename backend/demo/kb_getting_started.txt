TicketPilot Getting Started Guide

WELCOME TO TICKETPILOT
TicketPilot is an AI-powered customer support platform that combines smart ticketing with a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline. Your support team gets AI-drafted responses backed by your own documentation, and customers get faster, more accurate answers.

STEP 1: CREATE YOUR ORGANIZATION
After signing up, you'll be prompted to create your first organization. This is your workspace — all tickets, agents, and knowledge base documents belong to an organization. You can belong to multiple organizations (e.g., different clients or departments).

STEP 2: INVITE YOUR TEAM
Go to Organizations → click the invite icon on your org card. Enter your agent's email and assign them a role:
- Owner: full access, billing, can delete the org
- Admin: manage agents, KB, analytics — cannot access billing
- Rep: handle tickets, use the rep console, cannot manage team
- Member: read-only access to tickets and analytics

STEP 3: UPLOAD KNOWLEDGE BASE DOCUMENTS
Navigate to Knowledge Base → Upload. Drag and drop your support documentation (PDF, DOCX, TXT, or Markdown). The system will:
1. Extract and normalize the text
2. Split it into overlapping chunks (~2400 characters each)
3. Generate vector embeddings using Google text-embedding-004
4. Store embeddings in a FAISS index for fast semantic search

Best practice: Upload 5-15 documents covering your product FAQs, billing policies, technical guides, and troubleshooting steps.

STEP 4: CONFIGURE AI SETTINGS
Go to Admin → Settings to configure:
- Confidence threshold (default 55%): below this, tickets are flagged for human review
- Auto-escalation: enable to automatically assign low-confidence tickets to a rep
- Response tone: professional, friendly, or technical

STEP 5: TEST WITH A TICKET
Create a test ticket from the Tickets page. Open it and use the "Chat with AI" button to ask a question. You'll see:
- The AI response with numbered citations [1], [2] referencing your KB documents
- A confidence score (0-100%)
- An escalation flag if the AI isn't confident enough

STEP 6: MONITOR WITH ANALYTICS
Go to Analytics to see:
- Total tickets by status and priority
- AI confidence distribution
- Escalation rate (lower is better)
- Average response time
- Top ticket categories
